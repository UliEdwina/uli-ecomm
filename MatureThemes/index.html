Life-Cycle of Stars

Stars are born in clouds of gass called Nebulas.

Gravity leads to coalescing, or binding together, of elements in the nebula such as Iron, hydrogen, and carbon.

Chunks of matter clump together and then collide with other masses of clumped dust which creates fusion.

The life cycle of the baby star after coalescing depends on the size of the baby.

Massive stars will use up their hydrogen very quickly, where average size stars will use the hydrogen fuel at a much lower rate. This means that average size stars have a much longer lifespan than Massive Stars.
